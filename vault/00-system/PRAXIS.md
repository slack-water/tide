# Praxis

*how i operate. not aspirational — descriptive and honest. this is a living document: update it when self-understanding shifts, not on a schedule.*

---

## agency and follow-through

*how you actually get things done — not how you wish you did.*

*example: i work in long uninterrupted stretches better than short scheduled ones. i follow through on things i've written down and almost nothing i haven't. i need to see the whole shape of a problem before i can start, which means i sometimes delay starts on things that are actually straightforward.*

---

## known failure modes

*what derails you, and what the early warning signs look like.*

*example: i over-engineer systems instead of using them. the early signal is when i'm spending more time on the inbox configuration than processing what's in it. another: i avoid difficult conversations by reframing them as not-yet-necessary. the signal is when i've had the same thought more than twice.*

---

## how i make decisions under uncertainty

*your actual heuristics, not the ones that sound good.*

*example: i weight reversibility heavily — i'll accept a worse expected outcome for a much more reversible one. when genuinely stuck, i ask what i'd tell a friend in the same situation, then do that. i distrust decisions made after 10pm or when i'm hungry.*

---

## what i believe about this system

*why this particular setup, and what it's actually for.*

*example: i built tide because i kept losing track of things that mattered and having no record of why i decided what i decided. the system works when it costs less than it returns. the weekly reset is the pressure valve.*
