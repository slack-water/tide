# Praxis — example

*This file shows what a hydrated PRAXIS.md looks like. Delete it with `make init` when you're ready to write your own.*

---

## how to write this

PRAXIS.md is a description of how you actually operate — not how you'd like to, and not a list of habits you're trying to build. It's the honest version.

Write it in first person. Write it as observation, not aspiration. Update it when you catch yourself operating in a way you didn't expect — that's the signal that something has shifted.

**The sections aren't rules.** They're prompts. Fill in the ones that are useful; skip the ones that aren't. Add sections that fit how you think.

A good test: would a trusted person who knows you well read this and say "yes, that's exactly right"?

---

## example

---

### agency and follow-through

*how you actually get things done — not how you wish you did.*

i work in long uninterrupted stretches better than short scheduled ones. i follow through on things i've written down and almost nothing i haven't. i need to see the whole shape of a problem before i can start, which means i sometimes delay starts on things that are actually straightforward.

---

### known failure modes

*what derails you, and what the early warning signs look like.*

i over-engineer systems instead of using them. the early signal is when i'm spending more time on the inbox configuration than processing what's in it.

another: i avoid difficult conversations by reframing them as not-yet-necessary. the signal is when i've had the same thought more than twice.

---

### how i make decisions under uncertainty

*your actual heuristics, not the ones that sound good.*

i weight reversibility heavily — i'll accept a worse expected outcome for a much more reversible one. when genuinely stuck, i ask what i'd tell a friend in the same situation, then do that. i distrust decisions made after 10pm or when i'm hungry.

---

### what i believe about this system

*why this particular setup, and what it's actually for.*

i built tide because i kept losing track of things that mattered and having no record of why i decided what i decided. the system works when it costs less than it returns. the weekly reset is the pressure valve.
