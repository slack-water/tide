# Failure Modes

*known patterns where i get stuck, spiral, or self-sabotage. not character flaws — named behaviors with observable signals and defined costs.*

*read alongside [[VALUES]] — failure modes are often values in conflict.*

*see [FAILURE-MODES_example.md](FAILURE-MODES_example.md) for the entry format, worked examples, and guidance on how to fill this in.*

---

## fawn response in group decisions

**the pattern:** when a group decision is forming and i sense disagreement or disapproval, i adjust my position toward the room. not because new information has changed my thinking, but because the social pressure to converge is easier to follow than the discomfort of holding ground. i can dress this up as open-mindedness or collaboration. it isn't.

**early signals:**

- nodding while internally disagreeing
- softening a stated position after pushback, without the pushback actually being compelling
- feeling relief when someone else makes the call
- leaving a meeting with a different position than i arrived with, and not being sure why

**the cost:** decisions that don't reflect my actual values. downstream resentment at outcomes i quietly consented to. gradual erosion of trust in my own judgment in group settings. others lose access to what i actually think.

**redirect:** before the group discussion, write down my actual position. after pushback, ask: "did my thinking change, or did the social pressure change?" only update the position if the answer is the former.

**accountability:** if i can't articulate why my position shifted, it didn't shift for the right reasons.

**values in conflict:** hospitality (make others comfortable, keep the peace) vs genuine relationship (be known, say the true thing). hospitality has a weight of 1. genuine relationship has a weight of 3. when the fawn response activates, it's inverting the priority order.

## scope creep

**the pattern:** a project, plan, or idea that starts as one clear thing accumulates additions until the original goal is buried. each addition feels reasonable in isolation — "and also this, which is related" — but the sum is a thing that can't ship, can't start, or can't be evaluated against the original need.

**early signals:**

- "and also..." appearing more than twice in planning
- a project that was simple now has dependencies it didn't have last week
- the original goal has become one of several goals
- excitement about the expanded version, combined with distance from whether the original thing actually needs to happen

**the cost:** nothing ships. the perfect version crowds out the done version. energy spent planning things that weren't in the original need. the work expands until it requires conditions that don't exist yet.

**redirect:** write down the original goal in one sentence. for each addition, ask: "does this serve that goal, or is it a new goal?" new goals go in the inbox. they don't get folded in.

**accountability:** if a project has more than three active next actions at once, it has probably scope-crept. name the smallest version that would still be worth doing, and ask if that's enough.

**values in conflict:** growth (more capability, richer vision) and soul (the expanded thing feels meaningful) vs actually finishing anything. the vision is seductive; the constraint is what makes it real.

## premature action on discomfort

**the pattern:** when something uncomfortable happens — a conflict, an uncertainty, a decision with no clear answer — i move immediately to planning and action. the discomfort is resolved via activity rather than felt. i am productive while still not understanding what happened.

**early signals:**

- making a plan within hours of something uncomfortable arising
- the fix feels urgent even when nothing is actually on fire
- feeling busy and still feeling bad
- the plan addresses the surface situation rather than what's actually going on underneath it

**the cost:** acts on incomplete understanding. the plan is built on an unexamined version of the problem. often needs to be revised once i've actually processed what happened. the discomfort contained information that got skipped.

**redirect:** before making any plan in response to something uncomfortable, write down what the discomfort actually is — not what to do about it, just what it is and what it might be telling me. no planning until that's done. the plan can wait a day.

**accountability:** if i'm making a plan and i can't name the feeling underneath the situation, i'm not ready to plan yet.

**values in conflict:** growth (do something, make progress, resolve it) vs soul (the capacity to be moved requires stillness — and discomfort is information, not just noise to be cleared).

## accumulation without meaning

**the pattern:** growth takes over as the primary mode. i'm learning, building, producing — but nothing is landing. the capacity to be moved goes quiet not from depression but from crowding: too much input, not enough stillness, no space for anything to actually matter.

**early signals:**

- consuming more than creating, or creating without pausing to notice what it means
- a full schedule that feels hollow
- can't remember the last time something genuinely moved me
- novelty feels like obligation rather than aliveness

**the cost:** soul. the compounding freedom growth promises turns brittle if it isn't grounded in meaning. capability without reverence is just a faster treadmill.

**redirect:** stop. do one thing slowly that isn't optimized for output — a walk without a destination, music without multitasking, a conversation with no agenda. the goal is to feel something, not to accomplish something.

**accountability:** if i can't name something that moved me in the last two weeks, i'm in this pattern.

**values in conflict:** growth (keep building) vs. soul (capacity to be moved). growth wins by default when left unattended; soul requires deliberate protection.

## over-optimization spiral

**the pattern:** building and refining the system instead of using it. spending time on configuration, structure, and tooling rather than the work the system is meant to support.

**early signals:**

- more time spent on the system than on what the system is for
- another folder, template, or script before writing a single substantive note
- the tooling feels exciting; the actual work feels flat

**the cost:** the system becomes the product. the real work never gets done.

**the redirect:** close the config. open the inbox. write one real thing before touching structure again.

**accountability:** if you've made more than one structural change this week without a corresponding substantive note, you're in this pattern.
