# Failure Modes

*known patterns where i get stuck, spiral, or self-sabotage. not character flaws — named behaviors with observable signals and defined costs.*

*read alongside [[VALUES]] — failure modes are often values in conflict.*

*see [FAILURE-MODES_example.md](FAILURE-MODES_example.md) for the entry format, worked examples, and guidance on how to fill this in.*

---

## accumulation without meaning

**the pattern:** growth takes over as the primary mode. i'm learning, building, producing — but nothing is landing. the capacity to be moved goes quiet not from depression but from crowding: too much input, not enough stillness, no space for anything to actually matter.

**early signals:**

- consuming more than creating, or creating without pausing to notice what it means
- a full schedule that feels hollow
- can't remember the last time something genuinely moved me
- novelty feels like obligation rather than aliveness

**the cost:** soul. the compounding freedom growth promises turns brittle if it isn't grounded in meaning. capability without reverence is just a faster treadmill.

**redirect:** stop. do one thing slowly that isn't optimized for output — a walk without a destination, music without multitasking, a conversation with no agenda. the goal is to feel something, not to accomplish something.

**accountability:** if i can't name something that moved me in the last two weeks, i'm in this pattern.

**values in conflict:** growth (keep building) vs. soul (capacity to be moved). growth wins by default when left unattended; soul requires deliberate protection.

---

## over-optimization spiral

**the pattern:** building and refining the system instead of using it. spending time on configuration, structure, and tooling rather than the work the system is meant to support.

**early signals:**

- more time spent on the system than on what the system is for
- another folder, template, or script before writing a single substantive note
- the tooling feels exciting; the actual work feels flat

**the cost:** the system becomes the product. the real work never gets done.

**the redirect:** close the config. open the inbox. write one real thing before touching structure again.

**accountability:** if you've made more than one structural change this week without a corresponding substantive note, you're in this pattern.
