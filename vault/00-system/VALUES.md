# Values

*what i actually optimize for. upstream of everything — folder weights, project prioritization, and the review queue all derive from this.*

| value | weight |
|-------|--------|
| vitality | 5 |
| soul | 4 |
| genuine relationship | 3 |
| growth | 3 |
| hospitality | 1 |

*weights are relative, not absolute. all five are real. when values conflict, higher weight wins.*

---

## vitality (5)

the felt experience of being fully alive in my body. physical wellness, sensation, contrast, intensity, embodied presence. walking in the sun, the bracing cold of ice water after a sauna, the ache of tired legs. when i'm here, i know it.

**what it looks like in practice:** choosing physical experiences deliberately rather than by default. honoring what the body signals — rest when depleted, intensity when ready. not letting the day happen without having actually felt it.

**what violates it:** numbing out via screens and food. sedentary drift. the gap between knowing i need to move and actually moving.

---

## soul (4)

the capacity to be moved. by music, art, architecture, beauty, symbolic meaning, the interconnectedness of things across time and place. the cord of my deepest self thrumming when it encounters something that matters. reverence in the secular sense.

**what it looks like in practice:** creating deliberate space for beauty and meaning — not as reward but as necessity. noticing what moves me and following it, even when it's inconvenient. letting things land instead of processing them.

**what violates it:** filling silence with content. treating aesthetic experience as optional. grinding through without pausing. the slow hollowing-out that happens when growth becomes only accumulation.

---

## genuine relationship (3)

the reciprocal seeing. being truly known by others and knowing that i see them in return. not surface connection but real mutual recognition — the kind that happens in conversations that go somewhere unexpected.

**what it looks like in practice:** asking the questions that go deeper. staying in hard conversations instead of resolving them prematurely. being willing to be known, not just to know.

**what violates it:** one-sided connection — seeing others clearly but keeping myself opaque. using hospitality as a substitute for intimacy. performing presence without risking it.

---

## growth (3)

the continuous expansion of knowledge, capabilities, and creative agency. the thrill of novelty, building a unique combination of skills that lets me make things nobody else can make. not excellence for its own sake, but the compounding freedom that comes from becoming more capable over time.

**what it looks like in practice:** learning something genuinely new on a regular cadence. choosing projects that compound capability, not just demonstrate existing skill. building across domains, not just deeper into known territory.

**what violates it:** comfort-zone optimization. consuming information without making anything. staying where i'm already good instead of going where i'm not yet.

---

## hospitality (1)

creating space for people to feel safe, comfortable, and genuinely cared for. not just utility, but warmth. making people feel that their needs and desires matter and that someone is paying attention.

**what it looks like in practice:** remembering what people care about and acting on it. tending to comfort before getting to business. creating environments — physical, conversational — where people can relax into being themselves.

**what violates it:** transactional hosting. efficiency over warmth. being so focused on the agenda that the person across from me stops feeling seen.
