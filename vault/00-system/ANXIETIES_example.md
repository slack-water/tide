# Anxieties — example

*This file shows what a hydrated ANXIETIES.md looks like. Delete it with `make init` when you're ready to write your own.*

---

## how to write this

ANXIETIES.md is a tracker and challenger. The point isn't to eliminate anxiety — it's to look at it directly, ask whether it's real, and decide deliberately rather than react. Most anxieties shrink when named.

Each entry is a structured block. Add new entries at the top. Leave `resolution/update` blank until something actually shifts — don't manufacture closure.

**The challenge questions aren't rhetorical.** Answer them. Write the answers down. That's what makes the entry useful.

```markdown
## [title] — YYYY-MM-DD

**the anxiety:** [what you're worried about, in plain language]
**category:** decision-paralysis | relationship | health | financial | existential | other
**triggered by:** [what surfaced it — a conversation, a deadline, a quiet moment]

**challenge questions:**

- what is the realistic cost of reversing this decision?
- what is the cost of *not* deciding?
- what is the worst realistic outcome, and can i survive it?
- what would i tell a trusted friend in this situation?
- is this a real risk or a story i'm telling myself?

**resolution/update:** [leave blank until something shifts]
**status:** open | resolved | reframed
```

---

## example entry

---

## the system will become its own distraction — 2026-05-16

**the anxiety:** i'm going to spend all my time building and maintaining the vault instead of actually using it to do the things the vault is supposed to support.

**category:** decision-paralysis

**triggered by:** spending most of a day on folder structure and changelog format instead of writing a single real note.

**challenge questions:**

- what is the realistic cost of reversing this decision? low — it's markdown files, nothing is locked in.
- what is the cost of *not* deciding? the system never gets used. it just sits there, perfectly organized and empty.
- what is the worst realistic outcome, and can i survive it? i build a beautiful system that i never open again. yes, i'd survive it. it would just be a waste of a weekend.
- what would i tell a trusted friend in this situation? pick one real thing to write about and write it. the system can wait.
- is this a real risk or a story i'm telling myself? it's a real risk — i have a pattern of this. but naming it makes it smaller.

**resolution/update:** committed to writing at least one substantive note before touching the configuration again.

**status:** reframed
